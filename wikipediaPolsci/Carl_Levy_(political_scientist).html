Carl Levy (political scientist)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search

Carl Levy is professor of politics at Goldsmith's College, University of London. He is a specialist in the history of modern Italy and the theory and history of anarchism.

Education[edit]

Levy earned his BA from the State University of New York at Buffalo and his MA and PhD from the London School of Economics.

Career[edit]

Levy is professor of politics at Goldsmith's College, University of London. Levy is a specialist in the history of modern Italy and the theory and history of anarchism. In addition to Goldsmith's, Levy has held positions at Queen Mary Westfield College, the University of Kent at Canterbury and the Open University.[1]
